Sylvenie Lindor

The Sylvenie Lindor Foundation has emerged as means toward helping Children in need succeed through Education. Encouraging Francophone Youth integration to overcome obstacles via sports-Art and Education. Promoting integration of Visible Minority groups though Culture exchange.

HISTORY

The Sylvenie Lindor Foundation & Jean Marcelin Fontaine Multicultural Centre Inc. (SLF&JMFMC) was founded in Toronto in 2011 by Amikley Fontaine, a native of Chambellan ,Haiti . The SFL&CMJMF is a Non-Profit Organization. Amikley Fontaine names the Foundation after his late Mother Sylvenie Lindor . The Sylvenie Lindor Foundation is supported by Family Members ,Friends and the Board of Directors.

MANDATE

The mandate of the Sylvenie Lindor Foundation & Jean Marcelin Fontaine Multicultural Centre Inc. (SLF&JMFMC) is to work with youth of Haitian background and other Francophone youth to help them develop leadership skills to better integrate to Canadian life . Through its program ACT ( ACTION and CHANGE for a better TOMORROW) . The ACT program offers youth a clear vision of their surroundings and addresses how they can change it through training seminars, workshops, and debates and how to develop autonomy .

MISSION

The mission of the Sylvenie Lindor Foundation and Jean Marcelin Fontaine Multicultural Centre Inc. (SLF&JMFMC) is to work with visible minority groups: youth, adults, and different communities to aid in their integration, by exposing each culture to the other to initiate dialogue and intercultural exchange. In this context, SLF&JMFMC promotes multiculturalism as a main tool that creates a cultural mosaic in Canadian society.

OBJECTIVE

The objective of the Sylvenie Lindor Foundation and Jean Marcelin Fontaine Multicultural Centre Inc. (SLF&JMFMC) is:
 To work with Haitian and other Francophone youth to assist in their integration into Canadian life.
 To promote the integration of visible minority groups through art, culture, education, music and recreational activities.

Jean Jean Roosevelt

Author, singer, songwriter, guitarist and percussionist, Jean Jean Roosevelt is the figure of Haiti in the VII Games of La Francophonie. A native of Grand-Anse, in the southwest of Haiti, Jean Jean Roosevelt grew up in an artistic family, his father is a guitarist and his mother, director of a choir. At university, he specializes in the history of art. He has released three albums including Restart in 2007, in 2009 and Pinga Ya Danger in 2012.

Prior to the Games, the artist has won several competitions, including Radio Grand'Anse in 1990, Tropical Airways in 1998 and Solèy Sounds System in 2005 and 2006. His music address the themes of solidarity, gender equity, citizenship, perseverance, love, and preserving the environment. In 2007 he took the title of voluntary ambassador for Haiti's development.In 2013, Jean Jean Rossevelt participated in the seventh edition of the Francophone Games where he won the gold medal in the cultural song contest. Fun Facts

  • Soccer Tournament
  • Good night
  • Birthday Celebration
  • Canadian Tours
  • Monthly dinner

We use soccer as a fun way to help youth integrate into society. Their participation helps to develop networking skills with other youth from different communities.

This is a night where the Haitian youth and the Grenadier Fc soccer teams gather for a social evening. Good Night is held once a month. On that night the youth watch a movie or documentary together and reflect on it.

The Haitian Youth Movement celebrates the birthdays of each member. The youth board of directors plans the celebration.

At the Sylvenie Lindor Foundation, we organize Canadian tours to help youth understand Canadian history in a fun way! Visits to local tourist sites and museums are part of this initiative, as a way to better understand those who paved the way before us in a country that embraces diversity.

The board of Directors of the Sylvenie Foundation goes out for dinner once a month, as a way to socialize with people we work with and to show appreciation for each other.
